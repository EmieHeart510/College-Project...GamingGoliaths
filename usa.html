America's Gaming Industry

↑ Moving on Up ↑

The United States of America (U.S.A.) is the 2nd leading country in the global video game market, behind China.


In 2022, the amound of players reached an average of 209 million, with the U.S.A.'s population being over 300 million.

61% of the country's population plays a video game at least once a week.

Revenue

According to statistics, the country's video game market is projected to reach $78 billion in 2024, with a annual growth rate of 9.99%. Meaning, the United States will reach a volume revenue of $104 Billion by 2027.

Walks of Life

The United States is called the melting pot for its rich diversity. You will see video gamers everywhere regardless of age, gender, or racial/ethnic groups.

  • 53% of Players are Male
  • 46% are Female
  • Average Player Age in 2024 is currently 36
  • 11% of Players Identify as LGBTQ+

Most Popular Platforms

There are multiple platforms to choose from. Everyone has their preferences.

  • 67% Play Mobile Games
  • 53% Play PC Games
  • 36% Play on a Console
  • 12% Play Virtual Reality

American Titles

A lot of the video games that we've come to know and love, are made in the United States. There are many large game development companies that have left a mark in the world and continue to entertain the world with their creations.

Electronic Arts (EA)

Located in Redwood City, CA - EA is well known for making and/or publishing by these titles:

  • Mass Effect eeries
  • The Sims series
  • Apex Legends
  • Battlefield: Bad Company series

Bethesda Game Studios

Located in Rockville, MD - Bethesda is well known for making and/or publishing these titles:
  • Elder Scrolls series
  • Fallout series
  • Starfield

Rockstar Games

Located in New York City, NY - Rockstar Games is well known for making and/or publishing these titles:
  • Grand Theft Auto series
  • Red Dead Redemption series

Xbox Consoles

Xbox consoles have been around since 2001 and were founded by Microsoft, one of the largest IT corporations in the world. Xbox started out in direct competition with Sony's Playstation 2 and Nintendo's Gamecube.
