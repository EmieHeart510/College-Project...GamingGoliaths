Japan's Gaming Industry

Japan on the Rise

Japan is the 3rd leading country in the global video game market, behind China and the United States.

In 2022, the amound of players reached an average of 209 million, with the U.S.A.'s population being over 300 million.

56% of respondants have reported to play video games at least on one gaming device.

Revenue

According to statistics, the country's video game market is projected to reach $28 billion in 2024, with a annual growth rate of 6.78%. Meaning, Japan will reach a volume revenue of $34 Billion by 2027.

Walks of Life

  • 54% of Players are Male
  • 43% are Female
  • The Average Player Age is 36
  • 1% of Players Identify as Non-Binary
  • 2% preferred not to disclose their gender

Most Popular Platforms

Japan's most popular gaming platform are Mobile Games. With the highest penetration rate between the ages of 16-29.

  • 90% Play Mobile Games
  • 45% Play PC Games
  • 44% Play on a Console

Nintendo Consoles

Nintendo has been around since 1889 when the company first made hanafuda playing cards. However, their consoles first started in 1983 when the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) was first released. This triggered the interest of the American public, which lead it to being made available to the United States in 1986.

Playstation Consoles

PlayStation consoles made their debut in 1994 by Sony Computer Entertainment with the release of the first ever PlayStation (also known as PS One). After Sony had failed to release the PS One as the Super Nintendo Entertainment System - CD in the 1990s, they decided to make their own console. This new generation of technology raised Sony's popularity in the gaming industry, as the PS One used compact disc (CDs) instead of cartidges. The PS One was then made available to the United States in 1995.
